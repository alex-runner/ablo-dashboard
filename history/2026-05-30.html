UX/UI improvements

data-backed friction fixes · PostHog behaviour
Macro funnel beats every benchmark, so the wins are inside the steps — and signup abandonment and /studio rage-clicks are both trending the wrong way this week.
Reasoned by Claude over live PostHog behaviour · 7h ago. Re-thought every cycle — it can surface new friction patterns, not just preset checks.
UX · highSigned up
Make Google sign-in the default — the email link is leaking signups
Promote “Continue with Google” to the primary full-width button and demote “Email me a link” to a secondary option. The magic-link inbox round-trip is the single biggest avoidable drop in the whole funnel.
127 people opened the signup modal, only 45 finished (35%). 52 requested a magic link but just 3 ever completed a login event. “Email me a link” (64 clicks) is out-competing “Continue with Google” (47) for the primary slot — backwards, given Google needs no round-trip. · Watch session recordings ↗
People who abandoned signup, per day▲ getting worse
05/2005/29
UX · highTried the product
Fix the unresponsive control causing rage-clicks on /studio
Watch the /studio recordings and find the element that looks clickable but doesn’t respond (or is slow). This sits immediately before the “generate model” aha moment, so every frustrated exit here is a lost activation.
10 distinct people rage-clicked 30 times on /studio — ~17% of the 58 who entered the studio. No other page comes close. · Watch session recordings ↗
Rage-clicks on /studio, per day▲ getting worse
05/2005/29
UX · medium ✦ newTried the product
Steer users to the working import path, not the URL paste
The product-URL paste fails most of the time, but a different import path is clearly working — so lead with the photo/library import and offer URL-paste as the fallback, not the default. Also harden scraping for the domains people actually paste.
Of 11 people who pasted a product URL, 9 hit a scrape failure (only 4 succeeded). Yet 20 people imported a product successfully via other paths — the URL path is the broken one, not import itself.
URL-import scrape failures, per day▲ getting worse
05/2005/29
UX · medium ✦ newPurchase intent
Pricing interest isn’t turning into tracked checkout intent — verify the wiring
There’s real pricing curiosity but almost no recorded intent. Either the plan-select / checkout events aren’t firing (a tracking gap that would hide real revenue signal) or people bounce at the plan screen. Confirm pricing_plan_clicked / checkout_started fire on the live pricing page before reading this as a true drop-off.
“Pricing” was clicked 48 times and the credit/generate CTAs ~53 times, but only 2 pricing_plan_clicked or checkout_started events fired all campaign. That gap looks more like under-instrumentation than disinterest.
