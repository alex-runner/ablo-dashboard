UX/UI improvements

data-backed friction fixes · PostHog behaviour
Funnel is stable but the email magic-link round-trip is the single biggest leak (only 8 of 67 requesters complete login); the new TBS garment-builder flow shows a fresh 79% drop before users hit Generate.
Reasoned by Claude over live PostHog behaviour · 13h ago. Re-thought every cycle — it can surface new friction patterns, not just preset checks.
UX · highSigned up
The email magic-link path is where signups die — push Google as the only primary option
Make “Continue with Google” the single full-width primary button and collapse email to a small “use email instead” text link. The magic-link inbox round-trip is the dominant leak: people leave for their inbox and never come back.
235 opened the modal, only 83 completed (35%). Of the email path specifically, 67 people requested a magic link but only 8 actually completed login — a 12% finish rate. That round-trip, not the form layout, is the leak (mobile 39% vs desktop 29% complete, roughly even). · Watch session recordings ↗
UX · high ✦ newTried the product
New TBS garment-builder flow: 4 in 5 viewers never reach Generate
Watch recordings of the new TBS step and shorten it — collapse category→garment→sample into fewer required choices and make the Generate CTA persistent and visible from the first screen. This flow launched June 4 and now sits directly before the aha moment.
Since it went live (first_seen 2026-06-04), 173 people viewed the TBS page, 78 selected a category, 64 added a garment, 44 picked a sample, and only 37 clicked Generate — a 79% drop from view to generate-click in a 3-day window with solid volume. Signup abandonment also spiked the same days it launched (34 on Jun 4, the window high). · Watch session recordings ↗
UX · mediumTried the product
Rage-clicks on /studio keep climbing
Open the /studio recordings and find the control that looks clickable but doesn’t respond (or is too slow). It sits right before “generate model,” so each frustrated exit is a lost activation. The trend is worsening, not flat.
25 people rage-clicked 57 times on /studio (~26% of the 96 who entered the studio). Daily rage-clicks are rising again: 6 on Jun 5, 7 on Jun 6, 8 on Jun 7 — the highest run since launch week. · Watch session recordings ↗
UX · mediumTried the product
The product-URL importer still fails more than it works
Harden scraping for the domains people actually paste, and surface the photo-upload / manual fallback immediately on the first failure instead of after a retry. Lower-volume than the signup leak but a hard wall when hit.
13 of 17 people who submitted a product URL hit a scrape failure (only 6 succeeded) — a 76% failure rate over the window.
